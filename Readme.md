# Blocklists

A collection of custom blocklists (originally intended for Pi Hole) designed to improve privacy, block unwanted content, and restrict access to specific categories of websites.
Each list focuses on a different purpose, giving you the flexibility to choose exactly what you want to block.

## ⚠️ Disclaimer

These blocklists are provided as-is.
Some blocked domains may affect legitimate website functionality. Always review and adjust the lists to fit your personal needs.

### adult.txt
Blocks:
- Pornographic content
- Adult websites
- Online sex shops

Ideal for parental control or work environments.

### advertising.txt
Blocks:
- Advertising networks
- Third-party ads
- Pop-up ads

This list is focused on blocking domains used for displaying intrusive advertisements, reducing the amount of ads displayed on websites.
Perfect for improving browsing experience and reducing tracking through ads.

### games.txt
Blocks:
- Browser-based games
- Online gaming portals

Useful for reducing distractions or preventing bandwidth misuse.

### malicious.txt
Blocks:
- Malicious websites
- Phishing sites
- Domains associated with malware

This list aims to block domains that may pose a security threat, such as those known to distribute malware or engage in phishing attacks.
Essential for enhancing security and protecting against malicious online activity.

### miscellaneous.txt
This list contains domains that are considered unwanted for various reasons, but not specifically for blocking ads, tracking, or phishing. It serves as a general filter for sites that may be perceived as distracting or unnecessary.

Note that the domains included may vary in relevance depending on individual browsing habits and preferences.

### shopping.txt
Blocks:
- Unwanted Onlineshops
- Fake shops / scam stores

Useful to prevent impulsive shopping or avoid fraudulent websites.

### tracking.txt
Blocks:
- Tracking domains
- Analytics services
- Behavioral tracking

This list is designed to block domains that collect and track your online behavior, often used by advertisers and data brokers.
Ideal for increasing privacy by preventing the collection of personal data across websites.
